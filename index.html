FAQ

Questions, answered.

Where does the comp data come from?

From the OWCS broadcasts themselves. After a match finishes, an automated pipeline reviews the VOD, samples frames of live gameplay, and identifies the hero icons shown for both teams. Match results and schedules come from public OWCS results pages.

How is “win rate” defined here?

A hero counts as played on a map if it appeared in any recorded snapshot for that team on that map. Win rate is the share of those maps the team won. Comps change mid-map, so treat low sample sizes with care — the maps-played column is always shown.

What happens when the pipeline isn't sure?

It says so. Every detection carries a per-slot confidence score. Low scores are labeled needs review and stay out of the stats until a human confirms or corrects them. The stats only ever count reviewed or high-confidence comps.

How fresh is the data?

Comps are recorded from finished VODs, so matches usually appear within a day of airing. Live in-match tracking is on the roadmap.

Which regions are covered?

The goal is all OWCS regions — EMEA, North America, Asia, Japan, Pacific, and China — added one at a time as each broadcast layout is calibrated. The region filter shows what's live now.

Is this affiliated with Blizzard or OWCS?

No. This is an independent fan project. Overwatch and OWCS are trademarks of Blizzard Entertainment, Inc. This site records publicly broadcast information and links to official sources.
